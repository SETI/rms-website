Hi Mike and Mitch, I have had a look at the data throughout the week and I have not found any obvious problems.  I loaded about 40000 images spanning the entire data set (cruise and saturn) and viewed them as thumbnails (subsampled from the full images), and I also viewed a few thousand at full res.  I also read the NAC and WAC descriptions, which I found very useful.  The only issue I found there was a missing apostrophe in "don't"  in the same section in each file (lines 276 and 273 in the na and wa files respectively).  Finally I did some spot checks (a few hundred images) on the phase angles in the body and ring geometry tables and found good agreement.  I looked at phase angles because they are not very sensitive to precisely where you're looking on the body, and to what ephemeris is used, and it was a comparison I could do relatively quickly.  Note that I used the vicar headers and did not evaluate the xml headers.
