--- layout: base layout_style: default title: "Wellesley Workshop" --- [ ![\[RPX LOGO\]]({{ site.assets_url }}rpx/rpxlogo_t.gif)](rpxlogo.html) # A Workshop on Observations of the Saturn System from the 1995-6 Ring Plane Crossings ## July 25 & 26, 1997 Wellesley College Wellesley, Massachusetts The edge-on presentation of Saturn’s rings during 1995 and early 1996 provided an opportunity to study aspects of the Saturn system rarely observable from Earth. New results have been reported on the orbits and photometry of the smallest moons, thickness of the inner ring system and the nature of its atmosphere, structure of the tenuous outer E and G Rings, and determination of the rate of precession of Saturn’s pole. One of the biggest puzzles is the apparent shift of Prometheus in its orbit by 19 degrees. In addition, numerous observers have reported the discovery of new moons and/or ring clumps. The recent events probably provided our last chance to study many of these aspects of the Saturn system prior to the arrival by the Cassini orbiter in 2004. ## Program * [Program](program.html) ### Friday, July 25 * [Session 1: Overviews of Data Sets](program.html#session1) * [Session 2: The F Ring and Prometheus](program.html#session2) ### Saturday, July 26 * [Session 3: Main Rings and Photometric Models](program.html#session3) * [Session 4: Searches for Satellites](program.html#session4) * [Session 5: Faint Rings](program.html#session5) * [Session 6: The Future](program.html#session6) ## Workshop Details The meeting is timed to precede the 1997 [DPS meeting](//web.mit.edu/dps97/){:target="_blank"}, to be held at MIT beginning Monday, July 28.
